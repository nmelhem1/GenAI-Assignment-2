# Homework 2: Strategic Research Partnerships Workflow

## Business Workflow Definition
* **The Workflow:** Strategic Global Research Partnership Outreach. [cite_start]This involves identifying high-value institutional partners—such as university hospitals or biotech firms—and drafting bespoke collaboration proposals that align their current research gaps with Melhem Scientific’s cross-disciplinary platforms[cite: 154, 160].
* **The User:** Director of Global Strategic Partnerships. This executive is responsible for initiating rapport with Chief Medical Officers (CMOs) and Lead Principal Investigators (PIs).
* **System Input:** The "Partner Insight Packet." [cite_start]This includes the target institution’s recent publication themes, geographic focus (e.g., underserved regions), and a summary of their stated research objectives or funding gaps[cite: 167].
* **System Output:** A High-Level "First-Look" Proposal. [cite_start]A sophisticated, evidence-based formal letter that proposes a specific partnership model (e.g., medical education licensing or clinical trial coordination) and explicitly connects the partner’s needs to Melhem Scientific's expertise[cite: 168].
* **Value Proposition:** Precision at Scale. Initiating these high-stakes partnerships typically requires hours of manual synthesis per lead. [cite_start]Automating the "first-pass" proposal ensures that outreach is data-driven and professionally polished, allowing the leadership team to focus on high-touch negotiations and relationship management[cite: 169].
